What makes a hero in the tech world?

A hero in the tech world is someone who embodies the notion that communication over the Internet should never be censored and that people should be able to communicate as they so wish. A hero in the tech world also possesses an innate curosity and quick-witted nature in order to stay innovating with the swiftly evolving internet.

Aaron Swartz was The Internet's Own Boy. Aaron Swartz was a brilliant computer programmer and internet activist that strongly believed in Internet Freedom. Swartz was a strong advocate for open access to information, particularly academic research. He believed that knowledge should be freely accessible to all, rather than locked behind paywalls. Swartz was also a firm believer in fostering innovation and creativity through open sharing and collaboration. He co-authored the "RSS 1.0" specification at a young age and was involved in creating web technologies that promoted the sharing of content online. Swartz was additionally one of the early architects of Creative Commons and a developer of the Internet Archives' Open Library, a free book database and digital library open to the public.
